Introduction to the rally

Rally racing has a long history, with the first known use of the word "rally" in a road competition being in 1911 at the Monaco Rally. Here are some highlights from the history of rally racing: Origins The history of rally racing can be traced back to the origins of motorsport, including the 1894 Paris–Rouen Horseless Carriage Competition. Early years The first known use of the word "rally" in a road competition was the 1911 Monaco Rally. The Monte Carlo Rally is still held today and is part of the World Rally Championship (WRC). Popularity Rallying became popular after World War II in Europe and other places. Weekend rallies became common around the world. Evolution of cars The cars used in rally racing have evolved over the years. Some of the stages of the evolution of rally cars include: Group 4 (1973–1981) Group B (1982–1986) Group A (1987–1996) 1st-gen World Rally Car (1997–2016) 2nd-gen World Rally Car (2017–2021) Rally1 (2022~)
